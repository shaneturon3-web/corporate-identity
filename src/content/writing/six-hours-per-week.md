Solo providers lose billable capacity to accidental system integration: email threads, intake silos, docket drift, and the quiet tax of context-switching.

Industry copy often **overclaims** the drag — six hours *per day* sounds dramatic but fails external reality testing. The more defensible figure is roughly **six hours per week**: enough to hurt, small enough to recover with disciplined tooling.

## What recovery actually means

Recovery is not a dashboard metric. It is reclaimed calendar — blocks you can bill, think, or stop working without guilt.

A writer's discipline applies here too: measure what you can defend, publish what you can stand behind, revise when the ground shifts.

## Negative space

Not every hour needs automation. Some friction is judgment. The goal is to remove *accidental* integration work, not the craft of the profession itself.
